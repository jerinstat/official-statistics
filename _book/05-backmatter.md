# Glossary {-}

**Age-Specific Fertility Rate (ASFR):** The number of live births per 1,000 women in a specific age group in a given year.

**Balance of Payments (BoP):** A systematic record of all economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world during a given period.

**Census:** A complete enumeration of all persons in a country at a specified point in time.

**Civil Registration System (CRS):** The continuous and permanent recording of vital events (births, deaths, marriages) by government authorities.

**Consumer Price Index (CPI):** A measure of the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for a representative basket of goods and services.

**Crude Birth Rate (CBR):** The number of live births per 1,000 mid-year population in a given year.

**Crude Death Rate (CDR):** The number of deaths per 1,000 mid-year population in a given year.

**GDP (Gross Domestic Product):** The total monetary value of all final goods and services produced within a country's territory in a given period.

**GNP (Gross National Product):** GDP plus net factor income from abroad.

**Index of Industrial Production (IIP):** A composite indicator measuring short-term changes in the volume of production in the mining, manufacturing, and electricity sectors.

**Infant Mortality Rate (IMR):** The number of deaths of children under one year of age per 1,000 live births in a given year.

**Life Table:** A statistical model that traces a hypothetical cohort through successive ages, recording mortality experience and life expectancy.

**Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR):** The number of maternal deaths per 100,000 live births in a given period.

**National Statistical Office (NSO):** The apex body for official statistics in India, formed in 2019 by merging the CSO and NSSO.

**Official Statistics:** Quantitative or qualitative information compiled and disseminated by government agencies for public use.

**Sample Registration System (SRS):** A large-scale demographic survey providing annual estimates of fertility and mortality rates in India.

**System of National Accounts (SNA):** The international standard framework for national income accounting, currently SNA 2008.

**Total Fertility Rate (TFR):** The average number of children a woman would have if she experienced the current age-specific fertility rates throughout her reproductive years.

**Wholesale Price Index (WPI):** An index measuring price changes at the wholesale/producer level.
