Preface

This textbook has been written to meet the requirements of the Four Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUGP) of Calicut University for the course on Official Statistics.

The book is organised into four chapters aligned with the Calicut University FYUGP syllabus:

Chapter Title
1 Introduction
2 Population and Economic Statistics
3 Economic Development and National Income
4 Measuring Inequality and Poverty

Each chapter opens with learning objectives. Key terms are bolded when first introduced.

Acknowledgements

The author acknowledges data sources used in this book: the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI), the Registrar General of India, the Reserve Bank of India, and the United Nations Statistics Division.

Detailed Syllabus

Module I — Introduction (12 Hrs, 20 Marks)

  1. Introduction to Indian and International Statistical systems.
  2. Methods of collection of official statistics.
  3. Role, function and activities of Central and State Statistical organizations.
  4. Organization of large-scale sample surveys. Role of Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation (MoSPI).
  5. Central Statistical Office (CSO), National Sample Survey Office (NSSO), and National Statistical Commission.
  6. Government of India’s principal publications containing data on topics such as population, industry and finance.
  7. Scope and contents of population census of India.

Module II — Population and Economic Statistics (12 Hrs, 20 Marks)

  1. Population growth in developed and developing countries.
  2. Evaluation of performance of family welfare programmes.
  3. Projections of labour force and manpower.
  4. Statistics related to industries, foreign trade, balance of payment.
  5. Statistics related to cost of living, inflation, educational and other social statistics.

Module III — Economic Development and National Income (12 Hrs, 15 Marks)

  1. Economic development.
  2. Growth in per capita income and distributive justice; indices of development.
  3. Human Development Index.
  4. National income estimation — Product approach.
  5. National income estimation — Income approach.
  6. National income estimation — Expenditure approach.

Module IV — Measuring Inequality and Poverty (12 Hrs, 15 Marks)

  1. Measuring inequality in incomes: Lorenz curve.
  2. Gini Coefficient.
  3. Theil’s measure.
  4. Poverty measurements: Different issues.
  5. Measures of incidence and intensity of poverty.
